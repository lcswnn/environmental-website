Charities and Organizations

World Wild Life for Nature

The World Wildlife Fund for Nature (WWF) is a leading international conservation organization working to protect endangered species and their habitats across the globe. Through scientific research, community engagement, and policy advocacy, WWF strives to build a future where humans live in harmony with nature, addressing critical environmental challenges such as climate change, deforestation, and wildlife trafficking.

Greenpeace

Greenpeace is an influential global environmental organization known for its direct action campaigns and advocacy work addressing critical ecological issues worldwide. Founded in 1971, it focuses on protecting biodiversity, preventing climate change, ending nuclear threats, and safeguarding oceans and forests through peaceful protest, scientific research, and policy lobbying, while maintaining financial independence by accepting only donations from individuals rather than corporations or governments.

The Nature Conservancy

The Nature Conservancy is a global environmental nonprofit organization dedicated to conserving lands and waters on which all life depends through science-based approaches and collaborative partnerships. Since its founding in 1951, it has protected more than 125 million acres of land and thousands of miles of rivers worldwide, working in over 70 countries and territories to address the most pressing environmental challenges while balancing the needs of both nature and people.

Oceana

Oceana is the largest international advocacy organization focused solely on ocean conservation, working to protect and restore the world's oceans through targeted policy campaigns and scientific research. Founded in 2001, Oceana combines legal action, scientific expeditions, and grassroots mobilization to combat overfishing, habitat destruction, pollution, and the effects of climate change on marine ecosystems, achieving hundreds of concrete policy victories that help to make our oceans more biodiverse and abundant.

American Forests

American Forests is the oldest national nonprofit conservation organization in the United States, dedicated to protecting and restoring healthy forest ecosystems since its founding in 1875. The organization works to create resilient forests from coast to coast through large-scale reforestation projects, science-based policy initiatives, and urban forestry programs that address climate change, protect wildlife habitats, and promote environmental equity in communities disproportionately affected by tree cover disparity.

Alliance for the Great Lakes

The Alliance for the Great Lakes is a leading nonprofit organization dedicated to protecting and restoring the Great Lakes, which contain 20% of the world's fresh surface water, through policy advocacy, education, and community science programs. Founded in 1970, the Alliance works with scientists, policymakers, businesses, and thousands of volunteers to address threats such as pollution, invasive species, and habitat loss while promoting sustainable water management practices to ensure these vital freshwater ecosystems remain healthy for the 40 million people who depend on them.
