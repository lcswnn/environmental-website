Study: The ozone hole is healing, thanks to global reduction of CFCs

Updated: March 5, 2025 | Author: Jennifer Chu | MIT News

The article reports on a new MIT-led study confirming that the Antarctic ozone layer is healing as a direct result of global reductions in ozone-depleting substances. Led by Professor Susan Solomon and graduate student Peidong Wang, this research is the first to demonstrate with 95% statistical confidence that ozone recovery is primarily due to decreased chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs) rather than natural weather variability or greenhouse gases. The team used a "fingerprinting" technique borrowed from climate science to isolate the specific impact of reduced CFCs, comparing computer simulations with satellite observations from 2005 to 2018. This success story follows the 1987 Montreal Protocol, which was created after Solomon's 1986 expeditions confirmed CFCs were causing the ozone hole discovered in 1985. The researchers project that by approximately 2035, there might be years with no ozone depletion at all, highlighting how coordinated international environmental action can effectively solve global problems.
